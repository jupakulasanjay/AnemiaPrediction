ANEMIA
A very common medical condition in which the red blood cell count or the hemoglobin in the blood is less than normal
Anemia
Anemia is a condition marked by low levels of red blood cells or hemoglobin, vital for oxygen transport. Red blood cells and hemoglobin are crucial for transporting oxygen from the lungs to the body's tissues .
Cases on Anemia
Case 1
A 60-year-old male with a history of pernicious anemia presents with neurological symptoms such as tingling sensations in the hands and feet, along with weakness and difficulty walking. Laboratory tests reveal low hemoglobin and hematocrit levels. Serum vitamin B12 levels are significantly decreased, confirming a diagnosis of vitamin B12 deficiency anemia.
Case 2
A 35-year-old female presents with fatigue, weakness, and shortness of breath on exertion. She reports heavy menstrual bleeding for several months. Laboratory tests reveal low hemoglobin and hematocrit levels, along with microcytic and hypochromic red blood cells. Iron studies confirm iron deficiency, indicating iron-deficiency anemia secondary to menorrhagia.
Case 3
A 25-year-old female of African descent presents with jaundice, dark urine, and fatigue. Laboratory tests reveal low hemoglobin and elevated bilirubin levels. Peripheral blood smear shows evidence of hemolysis with schistocytes and spherocytes. Coombs test is positive for autoimmune hemolytic anemia, suggesting an autoimmune etiology.
